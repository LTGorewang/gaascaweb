ABOUT US GAASCA

Galeshewe Association for Care of Aged and Disabled (GAASCA) is a non-governmental Organisation and runs a Day Care Centre and Assisted Living Units for older and older disabled persons on a five day week basis. The Centre provides active ageing, developmental programmes, assisted living units and a well balanced meal on a daily basis. In terms of sustainability we mostly depend on the monthly subsidy from the Department of Social Development, fund raising and donations from some local business people, besides the monthly fees paid by the residents at the facility.

Our Organisation is a legal entity and we are in compliance with the Older Persons Act 2006 Act no. 13 2006 and registered as a Non-Profit Organisation with Non-Profit-Organisation Directorate and our registration number is; 010-965 NPO (certificate attached). The Organisation is managed by a governing board for a period of two years consisting of the executive (top five), two additional members, two beneficiaries representative and the Centre Manager as ex-officio. The Organisation also has a constitution that is guiding the operations of the Centre.

This Centre was established to perform active ageing through educational programmes, governmental programmes and activities that are relevant to the elderly. This project performs well by caring for the aged and disabled groups. The staff working with such groups are very passionate but, also go through stress due to lack of financial resources (lack of regular stipends)

The wish of management is to convert this facility to a multi-purpose Centre in order to extend our support to all vulnerable older and disabled persons and ensure a safe and secure environment for them. This is also the only facility of its kind in Galeshewe and due to its uniqueness, we request all funding Institutions/Organisation and individuals who have the interest of the disadvantaged older and disabled persons at heart to fully support this project financially in order to preserve the rights and dignity of the older and disabled persons.

OUR MISSION

To provide access to Primary Health Care and Social Services and implement Club Activity and Health Lifestyle programmes to Older Persons with Disabilities. To implement Educational and Poverty Alleviation programmes to Older Persons and Persons with Disabilities. To provide Temporary Emergency accommodation to abused elderly people and persons with disabilities plus assistive Living Units for Older Persons and Persons with Disabilities. To ensure the protection of the rights and dignity of Older Persons and Persons with Disabilities. To stay true to the objectives of GAASCA as stated in the Constitution.

OUR VISION

To render holistic and comprehensive services, in a safe and secure environment to Older Persons and Persons with Disabilities

ACHIEVEMENTS

In terms of achievement there are objectives that we have achieved, and some planned objectives for the organisation.
    Achieved Objectives
    • Establishment of a LAUNDRY in order to augment our limited financial resources.
    • Department of Social Development programmes that are related to older persons in conjunction with Age-in-Action and other stakeholders.
    • Provision of various active ageing programmes such as, exercises, spiritual upliftment sessions, cultural, handwork, sewing, beadwork and various sporting codes including indigenous games and golden games.
    • Medication and medical referral schedule to various health institutions in order to manage their health check up dates and collection of medication as prescribed by the health practitioners to avoid defaulting.
    • Provision of a comprehensive service to our beneficiaries living on their own, including affordable assisted living units and on time balanced meal on a five day week basis.
    • Provision of Adult Basic Education and Training on a four day week basis in conjunction with the Department of Education and, this program is also open to the public.
    • We also have in our fold retired professionals like, teachers, nurses, social workers ad pastors who are participating in our active ageing programmes.
